A001 a


Common meaning:
A word used before a singular noun to mean one thing, person, or idea that is not yet specific.


Example:
She found a book on the table.


RRGM bridge:
“A” marks one countable instance. In RRGM terms, it points to a single structure being selected from a wider field of possibility.

A002 an


Common meaning:
A form of “a” used before a word that begins with a vowel sound.


Example:
He gave an answer.


RRGM bridge:
“An” also marks one countable instance. The difference from “a” is sound structure, not meaning.

A003 able


Common meaning:
Having the power, skill, or condition needed to do something.


Example:

RRGM bridge:
“Able” means the structure and conditions are sufficient for an action to occur. MI may hold the identity or permission, but ES must provide enough working structure.
