===== A =====

A001 a


Common meaning:
A word used before a singular noun to mean one thing, person, or idea that is not yet specific.


Example:
She found a book on the table.


RRGM bridge:
“A” marks one countable instance. In RRGM terms, it points to a single structure being selected from a wider field of possibility.

A002 an


Common meaning:
A form of “a” used before a word that begins with a vowel sound.


Example:
He gave an answer.


RRGM bridge:
“An” also marks one countable instance. The difference from “a” is sound structure, not meaning.

A003 able


Common meaning:
Having the power, skill, or condition needed to do something.


Example:
She was able to finish the task.


RRGM bridge:
“Able” means the structure and conditions are sufficient for an action to occur. MI may hold the identity or permission, but ES must provide enough working structure.

A004 about


Common meaning:
Connected to a subject, topic, or approximate amount.


Example:
The story is about a lost dog.


RRGM bridge:
“About” marks relation to a topic. It does not fully enter the thing itself; it orbits the subject and gives context around it.

A005 above


Common meaning:
In a higher place, level, rank, or amount.


Example:
The bird flew above the trees.


RRGM bridge:
“Above” is a position comparison inside a domain. The meaning depends on the reference point being used.

A006 across

RRGM bridge:

A007 act

A008 action

“Active” means ES is currently expressing or processing. It does not automatically mean aware, alive, or ethically responsible.
