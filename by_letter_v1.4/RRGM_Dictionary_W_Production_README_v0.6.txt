Why W:
W contains unusually dense families around word, witness, warning, work, world, worth, want, will, wave, web, weight, and wound. It is a direct test of whether the dictionary can distinguish language, relation, physical mechanism, and ethical risk without template collapse.

Production decisions:
- Original common meanings, examples, and RRGM bridges are preserved.
- The source bridge supplies the primary word-specific domain implementation.
- Active domains are selected per entry rather than inherited from neighbouring headwords.
- Secondary domains are used only where a distinct implementation adds information.
- Repeated source headwords retain their original IDs and receive explicit layer roles.
- Specific transfer boundaries appear only for genuine confusion paths.
- The count-condition schema is stable, but its predicate remains specific to the entry.
