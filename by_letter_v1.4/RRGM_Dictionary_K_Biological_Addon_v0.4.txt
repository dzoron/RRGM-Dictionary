KI001 kelp

Common meaning:
A large brown alga forming marine growths or forests.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kelp is a multicellular brown alga whose holdfast, stipe, and blades support rapid growth in coastal water.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Kelp converts light and dissolved nutrients into tissue while creating habitat and altering local water movement.

Count condition:
Counts when the organism is identified as kelp and the claimed trait fits its species, life stage, and marine conditions.

Transfer limit:
Kelp is algae, not a vascular land plant.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI002 kelp-forest

Common meaning:
A dense marine ecosystem structured by large kelp.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kelp-forest is a coastal biological field in which kelp provides three-dimensional habitat, food, shelter, and flow structure.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Fish, invertebrates, microbes, predators, grazers, and drifting material couple through the kelp canopy and seafloor.

Count condition:
Counts when persistent kelp stands materially organize the local ecological community.

Transfer limit:
A few detached blades do not constitute a kelp forest.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI003 keratin

Common meaning:
A family of fibrous structural proteins in epithelial tissues.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Keratin is a durable protein structure supporting hair, nails, skin, feathers, horns, and related tissues.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Keratin filaments resist mechanical stress and differ by tissue, species, and developmental program.

Count condition:
Counts when the protein is identified in the relevant tissue or molecular record.

Transfer limit:
Keratinized tissues are not biologically identical merely because they share keratin.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI004 keratinocyte

Common meaning:
A major cell type of the epidermis that produces keratin.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Keratinocyte is an epithelial cell whose differentiation helps build and renew the skin barrier.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Keratinocytes divide in deeper epidermal layers, move outward, change structure, and contribute to the protective surface.

Count condition:
Counts when the cell is identified by epidermal position, morphology, markers, or lineage.

Transfer limit:
A mature surface corneocyte is a later state, not every keratinocyte state.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI005 keratin-layer

Common meaning:
A protective tissue layer rich in keratinized cells or material.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Keratin-layer is biological boundary ES formed by keratin-rich structures against friction, water loss, or exposure.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Its thickness, turnover, and permeability depend on body location, species, and environmental load.

Count condition:
Counts when keratinized material forms a continuous protective interface with measurable barrier function.

Transfer limit:
Thicker keratin is not automatically healthier or better.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI006 kidney

Common meaning:
A paired organ that regulates blood chemistry and produces urine.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kidney is a biological regulation organ coupling filtration, reabsorption, secretion, endocrine signalling, and fluid balance.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Nephrons process plasma-derived filtrate while kidney tissues help regulate electrolytes, acid-base state, pressure, and waste removal.

Count condition:
Counts when the organ or tissue is identified and the claimed function is tied to renal structure.

Transfer limit:
Kidney function cannot be reduced to passive filtering alone.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI007 kidney-cortex

Common meaning:
The outer region of a kidney containing many renal corpuscles and tubules.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kidney-cortex is the renal region where filtration units and major portions of tubular processing are densely located.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Blood flow, glomeruli, and cortical tubules couple to begin and modify urine formation.

Count condition:
Counts when the tissue is anatomically located in the renal cortex and distinguished from medulla or capsule.

Transfer limit:
Cortex location does not mean every renal process occurs only there.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI008 kidney-medulla

Common meaning:
The inner kidney region containing loops of Henle and collecting structures.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kidney-medulla is the renal region whose arranged tubules and osmotic gradients support urine concentration.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Countercurrent structure, blood supply, and collecting ducts shape water and solute recovery through the medulla.

Count condition:
Counts when the tissue lies within the renal medullary pyramids or related inner region.

Transfer limit:
The medulla does not independently determine whole-body hydration.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI009 kidney-filter

Common meaning:
The filtration barrier that begins separation of fluid from blood in the renal corpuscle.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kidney-filter is a selective biological interface formed by capillary, basement membrane, and podocyte structures.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Water and small solutes cross more readily than cells and many large proteins under normal barrier conditions.

Count condition:
Counts when the glomerular filtration barrier and its selective passage are the active structure.

Transfer limit:
Filtration is only the first stage of renal processing, not final urine composition.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI010 kinase

Common meaning:
An enzyme that transfers a phosphate group to a target molecule.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kinase is a catalytic protein whose substrate recognition and phosphate transfer change target activity, location, or interaction.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Kinases couple chemical energy and signalling by modifying specific proteins or other molecules.

Count condition:
Counts when an enzyme catalyses phosphorylation of a declared substrate under supported conditions.

Transfer limit:
The name kinase does not imply one substrate, pathway, or biological effect.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI011 kinase-signal

Common meaning:
A biological signal carried through regulated kinase activity.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kinase-signal is state change propagated by ordered phosphorylation and dephosphorylation events.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Receptors, scaffolds, substrates, phosphatases, and feedback determine where the signal travels and how long it persists.

Count condition:
Counts when kinase activity causally changes downstream molecular state in a measured pathway.

Transfer limit:
Phosphorylation correlation alone does not establish the full signalling route.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI012 karyotype

Common meaning:
The chromosome complement of a cell or organism arranged for description.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Karyotype is a chromosome-level record of number, size, shape, and major structural pattern.

Domain entry:
[BIO] It is produced from dividing cells or genomic methods and interpreted relative to species, cell type, and mosaic state.

Count condition:
Counts when chromosomes are identified and organized under a declared karyotyping method.

Transfer limit:
A karyotype does not reveal every gene sequence or functional trait.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI013 karyotype-variation

Common meaning:
Difference in chromosome number or large-scale structure among cells or organisms.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Karyotype-variation is chromosome-level divergence that may be inherited, developmental, mosaic, or disease-associated.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Gains, losses, rearrangements, and population differences alter the visible chromosome record.

Count condition:
Counts when the variation is demonstrated relative to a declared reference karyotype.

Transfer limit:
Visible variation does not determine its clinical or evolutionary meaning by itself.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI014 kinetochore

Common meaning:
A protein complex assembled at a chromosome centromere during cell division.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kinetochore is the chromosome interface that binds spindle microtubules and coordinates segregation.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Attachment, tension sensing, checkpoint signalling, and force transfer allow duplicated chromosomes to separate accurately.

Count condition:
Counts when the centromere-associated complex and its spindle interaction are identified.

Transfer limit:
The kinetochore is not the centromere DNA itself.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI015 kinetochore-fiber

Common meaning:
A spindle microtubule bundle attached to a kinetochore.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kinetochore-fiber is the force-carrying connection between chromosome kinetochore and spindle pole region.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Changes in microtubule length and attachment transmit motion and tension during chromosome segregation.

Count condition:
Counts when microtubules are shown to connect a kinetochore to the spindle apparatus.

Transfer limit:
Not every spindle microtubule is a kinetochore fiber.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI016 killer-T-cell

Common meaning:
A cytotoxic T lymphocyte able to kill recognized target cells.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Killer-T-cell is an adaptive immune cell that couples antigen recognition to controlled destruction of a target cell.

Domain entry:
[BIO] T-cell receptor recognition, co-signals, adhesion, and cytotoxic granules or death pathways determine the response.

Count condition:
Counts when a cytotoxic T-cell recognizes a target through supported antigen-specific mechanisms and triggers target-cell death.

Transfer limit:
Immune killing is recognition-dependent and should not be described as indiscriminate aggression.

[DEF]

KI017 ketone-body

Common meaning:
A small fuel molecule produced mainly by the liver during increased fat use.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Ketone-body is a circulating metabolic courier made from fatty-acid-derived acetyl units under low carbohydrate availability or other conditions.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Acetoacetate and beta-hydroxybutyrate can supply energy to tissues, while acetone is largely a by-product.

Count condition:
Counts when the molecule is identified as a ketone body and its production or use is measured in context.

Transfer limit:
Physiological ketosis and dangerous ketoacidosis are not the same state.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI018 ketosis

Common meaning:
A metabolic state with increased production and use of ketone bodies.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Ketosis is a fuel-state transition in which fat-derived ketone availability rises relative to ordinary carbohydrate-dominant conditions.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Hormones, liver metabolism, diet, fasting, disease, and energy demand shape the degree and consequence of ketosis.

Count condition:
Counts when ketone production or concentration exceeds a declared physiological baseline.

Transfer limit:
Ketosis alone does not specify cause, benefit, or clinical safety.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KI019 Krebs-cycle

Common meaning:
A central metabolic cycle that oxidizes acetyl groups and transfers energy to carriers.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Krebs-cycle is a repeating biochemical pathway coupling carbon oxidation to reduced electron carriers and precursor production.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Enzymes in the mitochondrial matrix or analogous prokaryotic space regenerate the cycle acceptor while releasing carbon dioxide.

Count condition:
Counts when the linked reactions and regeneration of the cycle intermediate are active in the organism or preparation.

Transfer limit:
The cycle is one part of metabolism and does not by itself equal total cellular respiration.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kiwi-bird is a ground-dwelling bird with reduced wings, strong legs, hair-like feathers, and sensory adaptations for nocturnal foraging.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Species differ in range, size, call, nesting, and conservation condition while sharing the kiwi lineage.

KI022 koala

Common meaning:
An arboreal Australian marsupial specialized for a eucalyptus-based diet.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Koala is a folivorous marsupial whose digestion, sleep pattern, climbing body, and pouch development fit a narrow ecological niche.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Individuals depend on suitable tree species, habitat continuity, water balance, and population health.

Count condition:
Counts when the animal is identified as Phascolarctos cinereus and the claim is population- and habitat-aware.

Transfer limit:
Koalas are marsupials, not bears.

A vigorous climbing vine in the genus Pueraria.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Kudzu is a fast-growing legume whose vines and roots can dominate disturbed habitats under favourable conditions.

A common name for several plants, including invasive Fallopia species.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Knotweed is a plant label that requires species-level identification before biological or management claims can count.

[BIO] Rhizome persistence, stem growth, reproduction, and habitat impact differ among knotweed taxa.

Count condition:
Counts when the plant is identified beyond the common name and the claimed trait fits that taxon.
