ZB001 zebrafish

Common meaning:
A small freshwater fish widely used as a vertebrate research organism.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zebrafish are transparent-embryo vertebrates whose rapid development supports observation of living biological processes.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Danio rerio develops externally, reproduces readily, and shares many conserved genes and organ systems with other vertebrates.

Count condition:
The organism is identified as Danio rerio and the claimed use or trait is tied to an observed life stage.

Transfer limit:
Results from zebrafish require a declared mapping before transfer to humans or other species.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB002 zebra-mussel

Common meaning:
A small striped freshwater bivalve that can form dense invasive colonies.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zebra-mussel is a filter-feeding mollusc whose attachment and rapid spread can restructure freshwater systems.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Dreissena polymorpha attaches by byssal threads, filters suspended material, and reproduces in ways that support large aggregations.

Count condition:
The organism is identified and its attachment, filtering, reproduction, or ecological spread is directly relevant.

Transfer limit:
Ecological impact varies by water body and should not be generalized without local evidence.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB003 zebra-finch

Common meaning:
A small social songbird commonly used in studies of vocal learning and behaviour.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zebra-finch is a vocal-learning bird whose song and social development emerge through coupled biology and experience.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Taeniopygia guttata develops species-typical song, pairing, and social behaviour under sex-, age-, and environment-dependent conditions.

Count condition:
The bird is identified as a zebra finch and the claim is tied to a declared developmental or behavioural context.

Transfer limit:
Its song-learning mechanisms are informative but not identical to human language.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB004 zooplankton

Common meaning:
Drifting or weakly swimming aquatic animals and animal-like organisms in plankton communities.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zooplankton are mobile consumers carried largely by water movement while linking primary production to larger aquatic food webs.

Domain entry:
[BIO] The group includes diverse larvae, protozoans, crustaceans, and gelatinous organisms whose size, depth, and diet vary widely.

Count condition:
The organisms occupy the planktonic field and obtain energy through consumption rather than photosynthesis alone.

Transfer limit:
Zooplankton is an ecological grouping, not one evolutionary lineage.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB005 zoospore

Common meaning:
A motile spore that moves through water or moist environments using flagella.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zoospore is a dispersal cell whose own motility carries reproduction or infection between suitable sites.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Certain fungi, oomycetes, algae, and other organisms release flagellated zoospores that swim, settle, and encyst or germinate.

Count condition:
A reproductive or dispersal cell is motile and its movement is part of the organism’s life cycle.

Transfer limit:
Not every spore is motile, and similar movement does not establish the same taxonomic group.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB006 zoosporangium

Common meaning:
A structure in which zoospores are produced or released.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zoosporangium is a bounded reproductive chamber that partitions living material into motile spores.

Domain entry:
[BIO] In relevant fungi, algae, or oomycetes, the sporangium forms zoospores and opens or extends a discharge path under suitable conditions.

Count condition:
The structure is observed producing or containing zoospores within the organism’s declared life cycle.

Transfer limit:
A general sporangium should not be called a zoosporangium when it produces nonmotile spores.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB007 zoonosis

Common meaning:
An infection or disease naturally transmitted between vertebrate animals and humans.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zoonosis is a cross-species disease relation in which a pathogen can pass between animal and human hosts.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Transmission may be direct, vector-borne, foodborne, environmental, or involve multiple host species and reservoirs.

Count condition:
A specific pathogen and a supported animal-human transmission path are identified.

Transfer limit:
Animal association alone does not prove zoonotic transmission.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB008 zoonotic-transmission

Common meaning:
The event or route by which a zoonotic pathogen crosses between animal and human hosts.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zoonotic-transmission is a host-boundary crossing carried by a pathogen through a defined exposure route.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Contact, droplets, vectors, food, water, tissue, or environmental material can carry the pathogen between species.

Count condition:
The source host, recipient host, pathogen, and plausible transmission route are all supported.

Transfer limit:
Temporal proximity to animals is not enough to establish transmission direction.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB009 zoonotic-reservoir

Common meaning:
An animal population or ecological system in which a pathogen is maintained and can seed human infection.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zoonotic-reservoir is the host field that preserves a pathogen across sequence independently of individual human cases.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Reservoir status depends on sustained pathogen maintenance, transmission ecology, and evidence linking the host population to spillover.

Count condition:
The pathogen persists in the identified host system and the maintenance cycle is demonstrated or strongly supported.

Transfer limit:
Detection in one animal does not prove that species is the reservoir.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB010 zygospore

Common meaning:
A thick-walled sexual reproductive stage formed after fusion in certain fungi and algae.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zygospore is a joined reproductive cell adapted to survive dormancy before germination and meiosis.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Compatible haploid cells fuse, form a diploid or paired-nuclear resting structure, and later resume development under suitable conditions.

Count condition:
Cell fusion produces the resistant reproductive stage recognized in the organism’s life cycle.

Transfer limit:
Do not generalize zygospore biology to all fungi, algae, or spores.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB011 zygotene

Common meaning:
A stage of meiotic prophase I during which homologous chromosomes begin pairing.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zygotene is the meiotic interval where homolog recognition becomes physical synapsis.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Homologous chromosomes align and the synaptonemal complex begins forming after leptotene and before pachytene.

Count condition:
Cells show the chromosome-pairing features used to distinguish zygotene within prophase I.

Transfer limit:
Stage boundaries are operational and may vary with organism and observation method.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB012 zona-pellucida

Common meaning:
The glycoprotein-rich extracellular coat surrounding a mammalian oocyte and early embryo.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zona-pellucida is a selective protective boundary that mediates sperm interaction and early embryo containment.

Domain entry:
[BIO] The matrix surrounds the oocyte, participates in species-relevant fertilization events, and remains until hatching before implantation.

Count condition:
The extracellular coat is identified around the mammalian oocyte or preimplantation embryo.

Transfer limit:
Its presence does not make fertilization or implantation inevitable.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB013 zona-reaction

Common meaning:
Post-fertilization changes in the zona pellucida that reduce additional sperm entry.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zona-reaction is a boundary modification triggered by fertilization to help block polyspermy.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Cortical granule release alters zona proteins and sperm-binding properties after sperm-oocyte fusion.

Count condition:
Fertilization-associated cortical activity produces measurable changes in the zona’s ability to bind or admit sperm.

Transfer limit:
The reaction is one block to polyspermy, not the whole fertilization process.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB014 zymogen

Common meaning:
An inactive or less active precursor that becomes an enzyme after a specific biochemical change.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zymogen stores catalytic potential in a form that limits activity until the proper activation condition occurs.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Proteolytic cleavage, conformational change, or another regulated event converts the precursor into its active enzyme form.

Count condition:
The molecule is shown to precede and produce a more active enzyme through a defined activation step.

Transfer limit:
Inactivity is relative to the target reaction and should not be treated as no biological function.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB015 zymogen-activation

Common meaning:
The biochemical transition that converts a zymogen into an active enzyme.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zymogen-activation is a regulated threshold crossing from held catalytic potential to expressed catalysis.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Cleavage or structural change exposes or forms the active site, often within cascades such as digestion, clotting, or signalling.

Count condition:
The precursor and active product are identified and the activating molecular event is observed or supported.

Transfer limit:
Uncontrolled activation can be harmful, so activation is not automatically improvement.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB016 zymology

Common meaning:
The biological and biochemical study of fermentation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zymology maps how microorganisms and enzymes transform substrates into fermentation products.

Domain entry:
[BIO] It examines organisms, pathways, conditions, metabolites, yield, and control of processes such as alcoholic or lactic fermentation.

Count condition:
A fermentation process is studied through its living agents, chemistry, conditions, and products.

Transfer limit:
Fermentation in one organism or substrate does not define every fermentation system.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB017 zymase

Common meaning:
A historical name for the enzyme system in yeast that carries alcoholic fermentation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zymase names coupled yeast enzymes that convert fermentable sugars toward ethanol and carbon dioxide.

Domain entry:
[BIO] The term refers to the cell-free fermentation activity demonstrated from yeast extracts rather than one single modern enzyme species.

Count condition:
Yeast-derived enzyme activity produces the characteristic fermentation products under suitable conditions.

Transfer limit:
Treat zymase as a historical enzyme-system term, not a single purified molecule.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB018 zinc-finger

Common meaning:
A compact protein structural motif stabilized by zinc coordination and often used for molecular binding.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zinc-finger is a folded interaction module whose coordinated zinc helps preserve a binding surface.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Different zinc-finger families bind DNA, RNA, proteins, lipids, or small molecules according to their sequence and fold.

Count condition:
A protein region has the characteristic metal-coordination and structural features of a declared zinc-finger class.

Transfer limit:
The name covers several structural families; one motif’s binding rule should not be universalized.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB019 zinc-finger-protein

Common meaning:
A protein containing one or more zinc-finger motifs.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zinc-finger-protein uses one or more metal-stabilized modules to recognize or organize molecular partners.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Such proteins may regulate transcription, bind nucleic acids, assemble complexes, or perform other interaction-dependent functions.

Count condition:
The protein contains a verified zinc-finger domain and the claimed function is supported for that protein.

Transfer limit:
Possession of a zinc finger does not determine one universal biological role.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB020 zinc-deficiency

Common meaning:
A biological condition in which available zinc is insufficient for normal organism function.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zinc-deficiency is inadequate trace-metal support for zinc-dependent enzymes, regulation, growth, immunity, or repair.

Domain entry:
[BIO] The condition depends on intake, absorption, loss, demand, binding, and organism-specific reference measures.

Count condition:
Clinical, physiological, or biochemical evidence shows insufficient zinc availability relative to the organism’s needs.

Transfer limit:
A low single measurement should be interpreted with method, inflammation, diet, and clinical context.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB021 zinc-toxicity

Common meaning:
Biological harm caused by excessive zinc exposure or availability.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zinc-toxicity is trace-metal overload that disrupts cells, organs, or the balance of other nutrients.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Harm depends on dose, chemical form, route, duration, species, and interactions such as impaired copper status.

Count condition:
A supported exposure path links zinc amount or form to observed or probable biological damage.

Transfer limit:
Zinc being essential does not mean every dose is safe.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB022 z-line

Common meaning:
The narrow line at the centre of a muscle sarcomere’s I band that marks the sarcomere boundary.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Z-line is the visible structural boundary where thin filaments from neighbouring sarcomeres anchor.

Domain entry:
[BIO] In striated muscle, repeating Z-lines define sarcomere length and transmit force through aligned contractile units.

Count condition:
Microscopy or structural analysis identifies the dense line bounding adjacent sarcomeres.

Transfer limit:
Z-line is often used interchangeably with Z-disc, but the line emphasizes the observed boundary.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB023 z-disc

Common meaning:
The protein-rich structural complex forming the boundary between adjacent sarcomeres.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Z-disc is the load-bearing assembly that anchors thin filaments and links contractile units to wider cell structure.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Alpha-actinin and many associated proteins organize the disc, transmit force, and participate in signalling and mechanosensation.

Count condition:
A sarcomere-boundary protein complex with the relevant anchoring and force-transfer role is identified.

Transfer limit:
The Z-disc is more than the thin visual line seen in microscopy; it is a three-dimensional molecular assembly.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZB024 zygosity

Common meaning:
The relation between alleles present at a genetic locus in an individual or sample.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zygosity describes whether copies at a locus are the same, different, absent, or present in a specified configuration.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Terms such as homozygous, heterozygous, hemizygous, and compound heterozygous are assigned relative to locus, ploidy, and variant definition.

Count condition:
The locus, allele copies, sample identity, and ploidy context are known well enough to classify their relation.

Transfer limit:
Zygosity at one locus does not describe the whole genome or determine phenotype by itself.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]
