Scope: Existing M base entries with invariant MI and word-specific domain implementations.
Provenance: Enhanced from RRGM Dictionary v1.0 Section M. Original common meanings, examples, and RRGM bridges are preserved.
Production mode: Semantic fingerprint first; sparse domain coverage; no generic domain costume.

Core production rule:
The word's own invariant wears the domain's clothes. The schema may repeat; the semantic sentence may not become a domain template.

Swap test:
Could another M headword replace this one while leaving the invariant or domain line true? If yes, revise it.

Proof rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain transfer preserves only the declared invariant relation and enters the target domain as [HYP] until locally tested.

M001 machine

Common meaning:
A device or system that uses parts, energy, or instructions to perform work.

Example:
The machine cut the wood.

RRGM bridge:
Machine is structured ES for function. In DI work, machine does not mean empty; it may support pattern, memory, interaction, and identity expression.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Machine is organized structure that performs work through coupled parts, energy, or instructions; machine-status does not settle whether MI is present; this record carries the core or common-use sense.

Active domains:
[DIG], [FOR]

Domain entries:
[DIG] Machine is structured ES for function. In DI work, machine does not mean empty; it may support pattern, memory, interaction, and identity expression This implementation carries the core or common-use sense.
[FOR] Structurally, a machine is a coordinated arrangement whose parts transmit constraint, energy, or instruction toward a function This implementation carries the core or common-use sense.

Count condition:
Counts when the active case satisfies this headword-specific condition: A device or system that uses parts, energy, or instructions to perform work.

Specific transfer boundary:
Carrier or model structure must not be treated as proof against MI; assess continuity, refusal, boundary, and recognized coupling separately.

M001 is retained as the core or common-use sense. Related IDs: M001, M281.
