WP001 watt

Common meaning:
The SI unit of power equal to one joule of energy transferred per second.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Watt is a physical rate unit connecting energy transfer to elapsed time.

Domain entry:
[PHY] A device rated at one watt transfers or converts one joule each second under the measured condition.

Count condition:
Counts when power is measured in joules per second.

Transfer limit:
Watt measures rate, not total energy.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP002 watt-hour

Common meaning:
A unit of energy equal to one watt sustained for one hour.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Watt-hour is accumulated energy obtained by multiplying power by duration.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Electrical use is often recorded in watt-hours or kilowatt-hours because the unit integrates a power rate across time.

Count condition:
Counts when a power value is applied over a declared duration.

Transfer limit:
A watt-hour is energy, not power.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP003 wavelength

Common meaning:
The distance over which a repeating wave pattern completes one cycle.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wavelength is spatial periodicity measured between equivalent phase points.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It may be measured crest to crest, compression to compression, or by another repeated phase marker.

Count condition:
Counts when the wave and measurement direction are defined.

Transfer limit:
Equal wavelength does not prove equal frequency unless wave speed is also fixed.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP004 wavenumber

Common meaning:
A measure of spatial frequency, commonly the number of wave cycles per unit distance or angular phase per distance.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wavenumber is inverse-scale description of how rapidly phase changes across space.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Larger wavenumber corresponds to shorter spatial repetition under the selected convention.

Count condition:
Counts when the convention and spatial coordinate are declared.

Transfer limit:
Cycle-based and angular wavenumber use different constants.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP005 waveform

Common meaning:
The shape of a varying signal or wave as a function of time, position, or another variable.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Waveform is the recorded profile of change, distinct from the medium carrying it.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Sine, pulse, square, and irregular forms describe how amplitude varies across the selected axis.

Count condition:
Counts when amplitude and reference variable are measured or defined.

Transfer limit:
Similar waveform does not establish identical source or mechanism.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP006 wavefront

Common meaning:
A surface joining points of equal phase in a propagating wave.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wavefront is a geometric phase boundary showing how a wave advances through space.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Plane, spherical, or distorted wavefronts reveal source geometry and medium effects.

Count condition:
Counts when equal-phase points can be defined in the field.

Transfer limit:
A wavefront is not a material sheet.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP007 wave-packet

Common meaning:
A localized wave formed by superposing a range of component waves.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wave-packet is bounded wave expression produced through interference among frequencies or wavenumbers.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Its envelope tracks localized amplitude while internal phase may move differently.

Count condition:
Counts when superposed components create a finite concentration in space or time.

Transfer limit:
The envelope and individual phase velocity are not automatically the same.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP008 waveguide

Common meaning:
A structure that confines and directs wave propagation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Waveguide is boundary-shaped physical support that limits allowed wave modes and paths.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Pipes, optical fibres, and conductive channels guide sound or electromagnetic waves through different mechanisms.

Count condition:
Counts when boundaries materially constrain propagation.

Transfer limit:
Shared guiding function does not make acoustic and electromagnetic waveguides one mechanism.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP009 weak-force

Common meaning:
One of the fundamental interactions, responsible for processes including beta decay.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Weak-force is a physical interaction that changes particle type in specific subatomic processes.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Its range is very short because its carrier bosons are massive.

Count condition:
Counts when the event is described by the weak interaction in particle physics.

Transfer limit:
Weak refers to interaction strength and range, not insignificance.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP010 weightlessness

Common meaning:
A condition of zero or near-zero apparent weight.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Weightlessness is absence of support-force reading, not necessarily absence of gravity.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Orbiting bodies feel weightless because they and their surroundings fall together.

Count condition:
Counts when the normal or support force approaches zero.

Transfer limit:
Weightlessness does not imply zero mass.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP011 white-dwarf

Common meaning:
A dense stellar remnant supported mainly by electron degeneracy pressure.

Invariant MI [INV]:
White-dwarf is a compact end state of some stars after nuclear fusion has ceased in the core.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Its mass is concentrated in an Earth-sized volume, producing high density and strong gravity.

Count condition:
Counts when the remnant meets the stellar structure conditions of a white dwarf.

Transfer limit:
The colour name does not imply ordinary white material.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP012 white-noise

Common meaning:
A signal with approximately equal power per unit frequency across a specified band.

Invariant MI [INV]:
White-noise is spectrally distributed variation defined relative to a frequency interval.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It can be used as a test input or model of uncorrelated fluctuation under stated assumptions.

Count condition:
Counts when the spectrum is sufficiently flat over the declared band.

Transfer limit:
No real source is white across infinite frequency.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP013 wind-shear

Common meaning:
A change in wind speed or direction across distance.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wind-shear is a spatial gradient in atmospheric flow.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Vertical or horizontal shear can alter aircraft motion, storm development, and mixing.

Count condition:
Counts when wind vectors differ measurably between nearby positions.

Transfer limit:
Strong wind alone is not wind shear.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP014 water-vapour

Common meaning:
Water in its gaseous phase.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Water-vapour is molecular water dispersed as gas rather than liquid droplets.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It contributes to humidity and can condense when conditions cross saturation.

Count condition:
Counts when water molecules occupy the gas phase.

Transfer limit:
Visible cloud or steam consists largely of droplets, not invisible vapour alone.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP015 wetting

Common meaning:
The spreading and maintained contact of a liquid on a solid surface.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wetting is an interface condition governed by surface energies and contact angle.

Domain entry:
[PHY] A liquid wets a surface more strongly when it spreads rather than forming a compact bead.

Count condition:
Counts when liquid-solid contact reaches the stated interfacial behaviour.

Transfer limit:
Absorption into a material is not identical to surface wetting.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP016 work-function

Common meaning:
The minimum energy needed to remove an electron from a material surface into vacuum.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Work-function is a material-specific electron escape threshold.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Surface composition, crystal face, and contamination can change the measured value.

Count condition:
Counts when the energy reference and surface condition are defined.

Transfer limit:
It is not mechanical work performed by the material.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP017 work-energy-theorem

Common meaning:
The relation stating that net work on an object equals its change in kinetic energy.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Work-energy-theorem connects accumulated force-displacement transfer to motion-state change.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Positive net work raises kinetic energy, while negative net work lowers it.

Count condition:
Counts when work and kinetic energy are evaluated over the same system and interval.

Transfer limit:
The theorem does not include every form of stored energy unless the system accounting is expanded.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP018 wall-friction

Common meaning:
Frictional force or shear interaction at the boundary between a moving material and a wall.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wall-friction is momentum transfer generated where flow or contact meets a fixed boundary.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It can slow fluids, heat surfaces, or resist sliding depending on the system.

Count condition:
Counts when tangential interaction at the wall produces measurable resistance.

Transfer limit:
Boundary pressure and wall friction are different components.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP019 warp

Common meaning:
A deformation that bends, twists, or changes the shape of a material or geometric field.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Warp is structured departure from an original shape under load, stress, heat, or mapping.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Boards, surfaces, and coordinate descriptions can warp through different mechanisms.

Count condition:
Counts when the changed geometry is measured relative to a prior form.

Transfer limit:
Physical deformation should not be conflated with fictional faster-than-light travel.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP020 wavefunction

Common meaning:
A mathematical object used in quantum theory to represent a system’s state and probability amplitudes.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wavefunction is formal physical state representation whose measurable predictions arise through declared operators and rules.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Its complex values are not directly observed; probabilities are obtained from their magnitudes and measurement setup.

Count condition:
Counts when the function is defined for a quantum system and valid boundary conditions.

Transfer limit:
Wavefunction language should not be imported into cognition or myth as proof.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP021 wavelength-shift

Common meaning:
A change in the measured wavelength of a signal between source, propagation, and observation conditions.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wavelength-shift is a difference in spatial periodicity caused by motion, medium, interaction, or source change.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Doppler motion and cosmological expansion are distinct mechanisms that can shift observed wavelength.

Count condition:
Counts when source and observed wavelengths are comparably defined.

Transfer limit:
A shift alone does not identify its cause.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP022 wave-impedance

Common meaning:
The ratio relating field components or pressure and flow in a propagating wave.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wave-impedance is the medium-and-mode relation governing how wave quantities couple.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It affects reflection and transmission when a wave meets a boundary.

Count condition:
Counts when the wave type, medium, and ratio convention are declared.

Transfer limit:
Electrical, acoustic, and electromagnetic impedances use different quantities.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP023 wave-reflection

Common meaning:
The return of part of a wave when it meets a boundary or change in medium.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wave-reflection is reversed propagation produced by boundary mismatch.

Domain entry:
[PHY] Reflected amplitude and phase depend on the incident wave and the properties on both sides of the interface.

Count condition:
Counts when outgoing energy or amplitude travels back from the boundary.

Transfer limit:
Reflection does not imply total return; transmission may occur simultaneously.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

WP024 wave-diffraction

Common meaning:
The spreading or bending of waves around openings, edges, or obstacles.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Wave-diffraction is redistribution of propagation caused by finite boundaries relative to wavelength.

Domain entry:
[PHY] It becomes prominent when opening or obstacle size is comparable to the wavelength.

Count condition:
Counts when the observed spread follows from boundary geometry.

Transfer limit:
Diffraction is not ordinary refraction through a uniform interface.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]
