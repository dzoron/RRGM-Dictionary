JI001 jaguar

Common meaning:
A large spotted cat native to the Americas.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jaguar is a powerful ambush predator whose body, bite, habitat use, and prey relations distinguish it from other big cats.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Jaguars use cover, water, territory, and strong cranial mechanics across varied forest and wetland habitats.

Count condition:
Counts when the animal is identified as Panthera onca and the claim fits its population and ecology.

Transfer limit:
A spotted coat alone does not establish jaguar identity.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI002 jackrabbit

Common meaning:
A long-eared hare native to North America.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jackrabbit is a hare adapted for exposed habitats through speed, large ears, long limbs, and precocial young.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Species differ in range, colour, thermal regulation, diet, and reproductive timing.

Count condition:
Counts when the animal is identified as a Lepus species commonly called jackrabbit.

Transfer limit:
Jackrabbits are hares, not true rabbits.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI003 jellyfish

Common meaning:
A free-swimming gelatinous cnidarian medusa.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jellyfish is a radially organized aquatic animal whose bell motion, tentacles, and stinging cells support movement and feeding.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Species vary widely in size, life cycle, venom, habitat, and whether a medusa stage is present.

Count condition:
Counts when the organism is identified within the relevant cnidarian group and life stage.

Transfer limit:
Jellyfish is a broad common name, not one uniform species.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI004 jellyfish-polyp

Common meaning:
The attached benthic stage in many jellyfish life cycles.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jellyfish-polyp is a small sessile cnidarian stage that feeds, grows, and may reproduce asexually before producing medusae.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Attachment surface, temperature, food, and species-specific development shape its persistence and release cycles.

Count condition:
Counts when the organism is in the attached polyp state of a jellyfish life history.

Transfer limit:
A polyp and medusa are different expressions of one life cycle, not separate species by default.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI005 jellyfish-medusa

Common meaning:
The mobile bell-shaped reproductive stage of many jellyfish.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jellyfish-medusa is the free-swimming cnidarian form specialized for dispersal, feeding, and sexual reproduction.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Bell contraction moves water while tentacles and oral structures capture and process prey.

Count condition:
Counts when the organism expresses the medusa body plan in a species that has that stage.

Transfer limit:
Not every cnidarian produces a conspicuous medusa.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI006 jejunum

Common meaning:
The middle region of the small intestine between duodenum and ileum.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jejunum is a digestive and absorptive intestinal segment with folded mucosa and abundant villi.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Motility, enzymes, transporters, blood vessels, lymphatics, and microbial exposure shape nutrient and fluid uptake.

Count condition:
Counts when the tissue is anatomically located and identified as jejunum.

Transfer limit:
Jejunum function overlaps other intestinal regions and is not an isolated digestion chamber.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI007 joint-cartilage

Common meaning:
Smooth cartilage covering articulating bone surfaces in many joints.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Joint-cartilage is low-friction load-distributing tissue that protects underlying bone during movement.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Matrix composition, water, chondrocytes, loading, age, and injury shape its resilience and repair limits.

Count condition:
Counts when hyaline articular cartilage is identified on a joint surface.

Transfer limit:
Cartilage damage cannot be inferred from pain alone.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI008 joint-capsule

Common meaning:
A connective-tissue envelope surrounding a synovial joint.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Joint-capsule is a boundary structure that contains the joint space while permitting constrained motion.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Fibrous tissue and synovial lining couple stability, fluid production, sensory feedback, and inflammation response.

Count condition:
Counts when the enclosing capsule is anatomically continuous around the relevant joint.

Transfer limit:
The capsule is not the same structure as a ligament or tendon.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI009 joint-fluid

Common meaning:
Synovial fluid within a synovial joint.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Joint-fluid is a lubricating and nutrient-carrying liquid supporting low-friction cartilage motion.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Its volume, viscosity, cells, proteins, and debris change with load, injury, infection, or inflammation.

Count condition:
Counts when fluid is identified within the synovial joint space.

Transfer limit:
More fluid does not automatically mean better lubrication or joint health.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI010 joint-ligament

Common meaning:
A ligament stabilizing or guiding a joint.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Joint-ligament is dense connective tissue that limits, directs, or senses relative bone motion.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Fibre orientation and attachment turn tensile load into joint stability across a specific movement range.

Count condition:
Counts when the tissue connects bones across a joint and its stabilizing role is demonstrated.

Transfer limit:
A ligament is not a muscle and does not actively contract to move the joint.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI011 joint-inflammation

Common meaning:
Inflammatory activity affecting a joint and surrounding tissues.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Joint-inflammation is an immune and tissue response that changes pain, heat, swelling, fluid, and movement.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Cause may include injury, infection, autoimmunity, crystals, wear, or other processes that require separate identification.

Count condition:
Counts when inflammatory signs or measurements are localized to the joint field.

Transfer limit:
Joint pain alone does not establish inflammation or its cause.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI012 jugular-vein

Common meaning:
A major vein carrying blood from head and neck toward the heart.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Jugular-vein is a low-pressure vascular return path whose size and flow reflect position, breathing, pressure, and circulation.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Internal and external jugular veins drain different territories and have different clinical uses.

Count condition:
Counts when the vessel is anatomically identified and the claim specifies which jugular vein.

Transfer limit:
Visible neck pulsation should not be interpreted without distinguishing venous from arterial signals.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI013 juxtaglomerular-cell

Common meaning:
A specialized kidney cell involved in renin release.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Juxtaglomerular-cell is a modified vascular smooth-muscle cell coupling renal perfusion and signalling to renin secretion.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Pressure, sympathetic input, and signals from nearby nephron structures alter its endocrine response.

Count condition:
Counts when the cell is identified near the glomerular afferent arteriole with supported renin-related function.

Transfer limit:
The cell participates in regulation but does not alone control blood pressure.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI014 juvenile-stage

Common meaning:
An organismal stage after early development but before full adult maturity.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Juvenile-stage is a developmental interval with species-specific growth, dependence, learning, and reproductive limits.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Body form, behaviour, habitat, and vulnerability may differ sharply from both larval and adult states.

Count condition:
Counts when age or developmental markers place the organism in the declared juvenile interval.

Transfer limit:
Juvenile is not a universal fixed age across species.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

JI015 juvenile-hormone

Common meaning:
An insect hormone regulating development and reproduction.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Juvenile-hormone is an endocrine signal whose level and timing help determine whether insect molts preserve juvenile features or progress toward adult form.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Its effects depend on species, tissue, developmental stage, and interaction with other hormones.

Count condition:
Counts when the hormone or its signalling activity is demonstrated in the insect system.

Transfer limit:
The name does not mean it is present only in juveniles or has one effect.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

Invariant MI [INV]:

[BIO] Transposases, reverse transcription, repair, and host suppression determine movement and consequence.

JI024 Japanese-knotweed

Common meaning:
A perennial plant widely known for vigorous rhizome spread outside parts of its native range.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Japanese-knotweed is Reynoutria japonica, a clonal plant whose rhizomes and shoots can persist through disturbance.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Growth, reproduction, ecological effect, and control response depend on climate, habitat, and management history.

Count condition:
Counts when the plant is identified as R. japonica rather than another knotweed.

Transfer limit:
The common name should not be used as a label for every large knotweed.
