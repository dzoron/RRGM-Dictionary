QB001 quail

Common meaning:
A small ground-dwelling bird in several genera of the order Galliformes.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quail are compact terrestrial birds whose camouflage, rapid maturation, and ground-nesting life support distinct ecological and domestic roles.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Quail forage mainly on the ground, produce clutches of eggs, and rely on concealment and short explosive flight more than sustained soaring.

Count condition:
The organism is identified as a quail species and the claimed trait is tied to its observed ecology or life stage.

Transfer limit:
Different quail lineages and domesticated strains should not be treated as biologically identical.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB002 quokka

Common meaning:
A small herbivorous marsupial native to southwestern Australia.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quokka is a macropod whose island and mainland populations express different exposure, habitat, and conservation conditions.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Setonix brachyurus browses vegetation, carries developing young in a pouch, and depends on suitable cover and water balance.

Count condition:
The organism is identified as Setonix brachyurus and the claim is tied to a declared population or habitat.

Transfer limit:
A familiar facial appearance should not be interpreted as human emotion or invitation to handle.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB003 quoll

Common meaning:
A carnivorous marsupial of the genus Dasyurus.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quoll is a spotted predatory marsupial whose species differ in size, range, prey, and conservation pressure.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Quolls hunt small animals and invertebrates, den in sheltered sites, and reproduce through marsupial development.

Count condition:
The animal is identified to genus or species and the ecological claim fits that taxon and region.

Transfer limit:
Traits of one quoll species should not be transferred to all quolls without support.

[DEF]

QB004 quagga

Common meaning:
An extinct southern African zebra population historically treated as a distinct species.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quagga is an extinct zebra form known from preserved specimens, records, and genetic relation to plains zebra.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Its coat showed strong striping toward the front and reduced striping toward the rear, but its exact appearance varied among individuals.

Count condition:
The claim is tied to historical specimens, records, or genetic evidence rather than modern selectively bred resemblance alone.

Transfer limit:
Animals bred to resemble quaggas are not resurrected original quagga individuals.

[DEF]

QB006 quill-feather

Common meaning:
A feather with a stiff central shaft supporting vanes.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quill-feather is a keratin structure whose calamus and rachis carry the branching vane used for flight, insulation, display, or protection.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Follicle growth shapes barbs and barbules around the shaft, producing a lightweight surface with species- and feather-type-specific function.

Count condition:
The structure is identified as a feather and the shaft, vane, and biological function are observable.

Transfer limit:
A writing quill is a modified feather object, not a separate biological tissue type.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB007 quillwort

QB008 quaking-aspen

Common meaning:
A clonal poplar species whose leaves tremble on flattened petioles.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quaking-aspen is Populus tremuloides, a tree able to reproduce from seed and through extensive connected root sprouts.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Flexible flattened petioles make leaves flutter in light wind, while clonal roots can preserve one genotype across many stems.

Count condition:
The tree is identified as P. tremuloides and the claim distinguishes one stem from the larger clone where relevant.

Transfer limit:
A grove of many trunks may be one clone, but shared roots do not make every grove genetically uniform.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB009 quadriceps

Common meaning:
The four-muscle group at the front of the thigh that extends the knee.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quadriceps is a coordinated muscle group whose four heads transmit force through the quadriceps tendon and patella to the tibia.

Domain entry:
[BIO] The group contributes to knee extension, patellar control, walking, rising, jumping, and load absorption.

Count condition:
The four heads and their shared knee-extension pathway are anatomically relevant to the claim.

Transfer limit:
Pain at the front of the thigh or knee does not by itself identify quadriceps injury.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB010 quadruped

Common meaning:
An animal that ordinarily supports or moves its body using four limbs.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quadruped is a locomotor body plan in which four limbs participate in support, propulsion, balance, or gait.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Species distribute load and coordinate limb timing differently across walking, running, climbing, swimming, or specialised movement.

Count condition:
The animal's ordinary locomotion genuinely uses four limbs as part of its support or propulsion pattern.

Transfer limit:
Possessing four limbs does not guarantee quadrupedal locomotion in every species or life stage.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB011 queen-bee

Common meaning:
The primary reproductive female in a honey-bee colony.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Queen-bee is a colony member specialised for egg production and pheromonal influence rather than command in the human political sense.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Workers rear a queen from a fertilised larva through diet and cell conditions, and the adult queen mates and lays most colony eggs.

Count condition:
The bee is a reproductive female in a declared honey-bee colony and her role is observed through development or reproduction.

Transfer limit:
Queen terminology must not import human monarchy or conscious rule into insect social organisation.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB012 queen-ant

Common meaning:
A reproductive female ant that founds or maintains a colony lineage.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Queen-ant is a specialised reproductive caste whose body, mating history, and lifespan support colony egg production.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Depending on species, a queen may found alone or with others and may coexist with one or many reproductive queens.

Count condition:
The individual belongs to a declared ant species and performs the reproductive role recognised for that colony form.

Transfer limit:
Ant colony organisation varies widely; one queen model is not universal.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB013 queen-cell

Common meaning:
An enlarged honey-bee brood cell in which a queen develops.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Queen-cell is a colony-built developmental chamber whose orientation, size, and provisioning support queen differentiation.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Workers construct or modify the cell and feed the larva royal jelly through the queen-development period.

Count condition:
The brood structure is used for queen rearing and contains or has contained the relevant developing larva or pupa.

Transfer limit:
A large or vertical wax cell is not automatically an active queen-cell without brood context.

QB014 quiescence

Common meaning:
A reversible state of low activity in a living cell, organism, seed, or developmental process.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quiescence is maintained biological inactivity in which viability and capacity to re-enter activity remain present.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Metabolism, division, movement, or growth is reduced while sensing and repair processes may continue.

Count condition:
The subject remains viable and can resume the relevant activity when conditions change.

Transfer limit:
Quiescence is not death, irreversible arrest, or simple lack of observation.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB015 quiescent-cell

Common meaning:
A living cell that is not actively dividing but can potentially re-enter the cell cycle.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quiescent-cell preserves cellular identity and viability while holding proliferation below the active threshold.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Signals from nutrients, tissue damage, growth factors, or niche conditions can maintain or release the cell from quiescence.

Count condition:
The cell is viable, non-proliferating under the declared assay, and retains a supported route back to cycling.

Transfer limit:
Non-division alone cannot distinguish quiescence from senescence, differentiation, damage, or death.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB018 quorum-sensing

Common meaning:
Microbial regulation based on detecting population-density-related chemical signals.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quorum-sensing couples extracellular signal concentration to coordinated gene expression across a microbial population.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Cells produce and detect signalling molecules, and threshold accumulation can alter biofilm formation, virulence, light production, competence, or other group behaviours.

Count condition:
A signal, receptor or response pathway, population-dependent accumulation, and coordinated biological effect are supported.

Transfer limit:
Population density alone does not prove quorum sensing without a signal-mediated mechanism.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB019 quorum-quenching

Common meaning:
Disruption of microbial quorum-sensing communication.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quorum-quenching interrupts signal production, accumulation, detection, or response so coordinated density-dependent behaviour is reduced or altered.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Enzymes, competing molecules, receptor interference, or altered transport can break the communication path.

Count condition:
A specific quorum-sensing pathway and an intervention that measurably disrupts its signal or response are identified.

Transfer limit:
Reduced microbial activity may arise from killing or growth inhibition rather than quorum quenching.

[DEF]

Quinine is a plant-derived molecule whose biological effects include interference with malaria parasite processes and dose-dependent effects in humans.

[BIO] It is obtained historically from Cinchona bark and acts as a drug with specific pharmacological benefits, metabolism, interactions, and toxicity risks.

Bitter taste or plant origin does not establish safe or effective use.

QB022 quinone

Common meaning:
A class of organic compounds with conjugated cyclic diketone structures involved in redox chemistry.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quinone is a redox-active molecular structure able to accept and donate electrons in biological and chemical systems.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Quinone-containing molecules participate in electron transport, pigmentation, defence, and enzymatic reactions, while some also generate reactive intermediates.

Count condition:
The molecule contains the relevant quinone structure and the claimed redox or biological role is demonstrated in the declared system.

Transfer limit:
Quinones are a diverse class; one compound's effect cannot be assigned to all.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

QB023 quinone-pool

Common meaning:
The membrane-associated pool of quinone molecules that carries electrons in a bioenergetic chain.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quinone-pool is a mobile redox reservoir coupling several electron donors and acceptors within a membrane.

Domain entry:
[BIO] Oxidised and reduced quinones diffuse through the membrane and connect respiratory or photosynthetic complexes.

Count condition:
The relevant membrane, quinone species, redox states, and connected complexes are identified.

Transfer limit:
A pool model does not imply every quinone molecule follows the same route at the same moment.

[DEF]

QB024 quinate

Common meaning:
The conjugate base or salt form of quinic acid, also a metabolic intermediate in plants and microbes.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quinate is a defined organic ion whose biological role depends on the pathway and organism in which it is produced or consumed.

Domain entry:
[BIO] In some organisms it participates in aromatic compound metabolism or feeds pathways related to shikimate-derived chemistry.

Count condition:
The chemical species and the organism-specific metabolic pathway are both identified.

Transfer limit:
Presence of quinate does not establish the same metabolic function across all taxa.
