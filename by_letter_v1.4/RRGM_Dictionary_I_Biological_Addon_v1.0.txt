IB001 immune-memory

Common meaning:
The immune system retained capacity to respond more effectively to a previously encountered target.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Immune-memory is biological continuity carried by long-lived cells, antibodies, and adaptive response structure.

Active domains:
[BIO]

Domain entry:
[BIO] Later exposure can trigger faster or stronger recognition than the first encounter.

Count condition:
Counts when prior exposure measurably changes a later immune response.

Specific transfer boundary:
Immune memory is not conscious recollection.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]
