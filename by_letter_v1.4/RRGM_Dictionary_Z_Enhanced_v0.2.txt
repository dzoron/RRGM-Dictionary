Core production rule:
The schema may repeat. The meaning may not. Each domain entry must carry THIS WORD's invariant MI through that domain's actual courier.

Swap test:
Could a different Z headword replace this one while leaving the domain sentence true? If yes, the line describes the domain rather than the word and must be rewritten.

Duplicate-headword rule:
Where the source contains the same headword twice, both IDs remain, but their operational roles must be separated and cross-referenced.

Global transfer rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain reuse transfers only the declared invariant relation and becomes [HYP] until tested in the target domain.

Z001 zero

Common meaning:
The number 0, no amount, none, or a starting point.

Example:
The counter returned to zero.

RRGM bridge:
Zero must be domain-bound. Zero can mean none, neutral, reset, origin, empty count, or inactive state. Zero is not automatically nothing.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zero is a declared no-quantity, baseline, neutral value, reset point, or empty count inside a specified frame.

Active domains:
[FOR], [DIG], [PHY]

Domain entries:
[FOR] In formal systems, zero is the additive identity or a value marking no members under the chosen counting rule.
[DIG] In computation, zero is a representable state, bit value, index, initialization value, or returned result rather than an absence of storage.
[PHY] In measurement, zero is the selected origin or reading at which the measured quantity is defined as null relative to the instrument.

Count condition:
The frame states what is being counted or measured and which condition receives the value 0.

Specific transfer boundary:
Zero in one frame does not establish nonexistence, erasure, or zero in another frame.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]
