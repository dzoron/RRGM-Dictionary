KY001 ka

Common meaning:
An ancient Egyptian concept of a life-force or enduring vital double.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Ka is the mythic life-presence that remains related to a person beyond visible bodily activity.

Domain entry:
[MYT] Ka carries continued identity, nourishment duty, and recognition between the living, the dead, and the preserved name.

Count condition:
Counts when the story or rite distinguishes this continuing life-presence from the body and gives it relational consequence.

Transfer limit:
Ka belongs to a specific Egyptian religious frame and should not be treated as a universal soul-mechanism.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KY002 kami

Invariant MI [INV]:

Domain entry:
[MYT] Kami enters count through reverence, shrine, story, ritual, or experienced presence without requiring one uniform bodily form.

Count condition:
Counts when the active tradition recognizes a particular sacred presence and its relation to community or place.

Transfer limit:
Kami should not be flattened into a generic Western god category.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

KY003 karma

Common meaning:
A moral-causal pattern in which action carries consequences across a life path or sequence of lives.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Karma is the mythic record that binds action, consequence, and continuing responsibility across sequence.

Domain entry:
[MYT] Karma turns deeds, motives, and attachments into later conditions without reducing the pattern to immediate reward or punishment.

Count condition:
Counts when a tradition or narrative explicitly links prior action to later moral or existential consequence.

Transfer limit:
Karma is not shorthand for instant revenge or proof that suffering was deserved.

[DEF]

KY005 kenosis

Invariant MI [INV]:
