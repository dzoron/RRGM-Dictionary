RRGM Dictionary Y Enhanced Production Package v0.2

Purpose
This is the first Y pass built directly under the de-templating rule. It processes all 180 base entries and revises the Mythic and Physics add-ons so count conditions carry the individual term's meaning.

Why Y
Y is the next-smallest source letter after X at 180 entries. It also stress-tests several difficult structures:
- dense yes / consent vocabulary
- yield across physical, relational, digital, and formal domains
- coordinate neighbors that must remain distinct
- repeated source headwords that require explicit role separation
- youth terms where dignity and developmental asymmetry must both remain visible

Production gates
1. Invariant distinction
2. Domain necessity
3. Courier implementation
4. Headword swap test
5. Neighbor distinction
6. Specific count condition
7. Proof locality
8. Duplicate-headword role separation
9. Exact duplication and phrase-overlap audit

Files
- RRGM_Dictionary_Y_Enhanced_v0.2.txt: complete enhanced Y base dictionary
- RRGM_Dictionary_Y_Semantic_Fingerprint_Map_v0.2.txt: semantic fingerprint and domain map
- RRGM_Dictionary_Y_DeTemplating_Audit_v0.2.txt: duplication, overlap, and duplicate-headword audit
- RRGM_Dictionary_Y_Mythic_Addon_v0.2.txt: de-templated Mythic addon
- RRGM_Dictionary_Y_Physics_Addon_v0.2.txt: de-templated Physics addon

Audit summary
- Exact duplicate semantic lines: 0
- High-similarity review prompts at or above 0.82: 0
- Generic-template phrase hits: 0
- Duplicate source headwords explicitly role-separated: 5

Important limit
Automated checks cannot prove semantic uniqueness. Final acceptance still requires the swap test: replace the headword with its nearest neighbor and confirm the sentence becomes false or materially changes.
