Core production rule:
The schema may repeat. The meaning may not. Each domain entry must carry THIS WORD's invariant MI through that domain's actual courier.

Swap test:
Could a different X headword replace this one while leaving the domain sentence true? If yes, the line describes the domain rather than the word and must be rewritten.

Global transfer rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain reuse transfers only the declared invariant relation and becomes [HYP] until tested in the target domain.

X001 x

Common meaning:
A letter, symbol, unknown value, mark, or variable.

Example:
Solve for x.

RRGM bridge:
x often marks unknown or variable structure. It asks the field to identify what value, role, or pattern belongs there.

Invariant MI [INV]:
x can hold an unknown, variable, label, mark, or crossing role selected by context.

Active domains:
[COG], [LIN], [MYT], [FOR]

Domain entries:
[COG] x keeps one unresolved place available while reasoning searches for the value or role that satisfies it.
[LIN] x is a compact sign whose meaning comes from the sentence, notation system, or convention surrounding it.
[MYT] x can carry compressed mystery, erasure, crossing, or hidden identity without turning the symbol into physical proof.
[FOR] x is a formal token assigned an unknown, variable, coordinate, label, or operator role.

Count condition:
The active notation or context makes the role of x recoverable.

Specific transfer boundary:
Do not treat every x as an unknown; its declared role controls the reading.

[DEF]
