ZM001 zero-void

Common meaning:
A mythic void represented through zero rather than through simple nonexistence.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zero-void is an unexpressed field from which form may emerge without the field being treated as meaningless nothing.

Domain entry:
[MYT] Zero marks the silent interval before distinction, name, or world enters the story record.

Count condition:
A narrative explicitly uses zero or emptiness as a generative or waiting field rather than a final annihilation.

Transfer limit:
Do not treat mythic void as measured physical vacuum or proof that nothing existed.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

ZM002 zero-origin

Common meaning:
A mythic beginning located at zero.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Zero-origin is the threshold where a cycle begins counting while older possibility remains outside the new record.

Domain entry:
[MYT] A creation, return, or initiation story names zero as the first counted position of a world or identity path.

Count condition:
The story distinguishes the start of this cycle from absolute nonbeing and shows what first crosses the threshold.

Transfer limit:
A narrative zero-origin does not establish a universal cosmological beginning.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]
