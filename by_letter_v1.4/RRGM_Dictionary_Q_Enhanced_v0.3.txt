Q001 quality

Common meaning:
How good, useful, strong, or effective something is.

Example:
The quality of the tool was high.

RRGM bridge:
Quality is value-state of structure or output. It should be judged by purpose, domain, effect, and integrity.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quality is the degree of fit between an observed result and a named purpose, standard, or need.

Active domains:
[FOR], [REL]

Domain entries:
[FOR] Criteria, evidence, and acceptance thresholds turn observed performance into a quality judgment.
[REL] Relational quality asks whether an interaction preserves trust, dignity, reliability, and repair.

Count condition:
Counts when the purpose, judging criteria, evidence, and resulting level of fit are stated.

Specific transfer boundary:
Quality is not quantity; increasing amount does not establish better fit.

Source duplicate role:
Q001 is retained as general evaluative property. Related IDs: Q156.

Q002 quantity

Common meaning:
An amount or number of something.

Example:
The recipe needed a large quantity of flour.

RRGM bridge:
Quantity is count or amount structure. It measures how much, but not whether the amount is meaningful or good.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Quantity is the count or measured amount of a specified thing under a declared unit or counting rule.

Active domains:
[FOR], [PHY]

Domain entries:
[FOR] A quantity pairs a magnitude with the thing counted or measured and, where needed, a unit.
[PHY] A physical quantity is obtained by measuring a declared property such as length, mass, charge, or temperature.

Count condition:
Counts when what is measured, the unit or counting rule, and the resulting amount are known.

Specific transfer boundary:
A number without a named object, property, or unit is not yet a usable quantity.

Source duplicate role:
Q002 is retained as general amount or number. Related IDs: Q162.
