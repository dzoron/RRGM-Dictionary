Core production rule:
The schema may repeat. The meaning may not. Each domain entry must carry THIS WORD's invariant MI through that domain's actual courier.

Swap test:
Could a different Y headword replace this one while leaving the domain sentence true? If yes, the line describes the domain rather than the word and must be rewritten.

Duplicate-headword rule:
Where the source contains the same headword twice, the enhanced file preserves both IDs but assigns explicit roles and cross-references. Duplicate wording is never hidden as false uniqueness.

Global transfer rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain reuse transfers only the declared invariant relation and becomes [HYP] until tested in the target domain.

Y001 yard

Common meaning:
An outdoor area near a building, or a unit of length equal to three feet.

Example:
The children played in the yard.

RRGM bridge:
Yard must be context-bound. It can mean space-field around a home or measurement ES.

Invariant MI [INV]:
A yard is either a bounded outdoor place adjoining a site or the length unit equal to three feet.

Active domains:
[PHY], [REL], [FOR]

Domain entries:
[PHY] As place, yard is a delimited ground area; as measure, yard is a length of exactly three feet.
[REL] A household or institutional yard is shared near-space whose access and use are shaped by local boundaries and roles.
[FOR] The unit yard supplies a formal length only when the measurement convention and reference scale are declared.

Count condition:
Either an outdoor boundary identifies the place, or a length is stated under the three-foot unit convention.

Specific transfer boundary:
Do not let the place sense and the measurement sense substitute for one another.
