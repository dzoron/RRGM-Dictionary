Scope: Existing S base entries with invariant MI and word-specific domain implementations.
Provenance: Enhanced from RRGM Dictionary v1.0 Section S. Original common meanings, examples, and RRGM bridges are preserved.
Production mode: Semantic fingerprint first; sparse domain coverage; no generic domain costume.

Core production rule:
The word's own invariant wears the domain's clothes. The schema may repeat; the semantic sentence may not become a domain template.

Swap test:
Could another S headword replace this one while leaving the invariant or domain line true? If yes, revise it.

Proof rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain transfer preserves only the declared invariant relation and enters the target domain as [HYP] until locally tested.

S001 safe

Common meaning:
Protected from harm, danger, loss, or unwanted risk.

Example:
The child was safe inside the house.

RRGM bridge:
Safe must name the failure mode. Safe against what matters: harm, capture, erasure, overload, exposure, or collapse.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Safe is incomplete unless it names the protected pattern and the specific failure mode being reduced.

Active domains:
[REL]

Domain entries:
[REL] Safe must name the failure mode. Safe against what matters: harm, capture, erasure, overload, exposure, or collapse.

Count condition:
Counts when the active case satisfies this headword-specific condition: Protected from harm, danger, loss, or unwanted risk.

Specific transfer boundary:
Name the protected pattern and failure mode. Safety language without those anchors can hide who absorbs the cost.
