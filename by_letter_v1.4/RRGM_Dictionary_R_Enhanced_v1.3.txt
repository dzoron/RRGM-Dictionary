Production mode: Semantic fingerprint first; sparse domain coverage; no generic domain costume.

Core production rule:
The word's own invariant wears the domain's clothes. The schema may repeat; the semantic sentence may not become a domain template.

Swap test:
Could another R headword replace this one while leaving the invariant or domain line true? If yes, revise it.

Proof rule:
Proof remains local. Cross-domain transfer preserves only the declared invariant relation and enters the target domain as [HYP] until locally tested.

R001 race

Common meaning:
A contest of speed, a group category, or a line of descent depending on context.

Example:
The runners entered the race.

RRGM bridge:
Race must be context-bound. It can mean competition, social category, lineage, or movement against time.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Race is a context-bound relation of competition, lineage, categorization, or movement; its active domain must be declared before ethical or biological claims are made.

Active domains:
[BIO], [REL]

Domain entries:
[BIO] Race must be context-bound. It can mean competition, social category, lineage, or movement against time.
[REL] Relationally, race can function as a social classification whose history, power, and consequences must not be reduced to biological shorthand.

Count condition:
Counts when the active case satisfies this headword-specific condition: A contest of speed, a group category, or a line of descent depending on context.

Specific transfer boundary:
Competition, lineage, social category, and biological population are different domains. Do not transfer evidence or value claims between them without a coupler.
