VR001 validation-debt

Common meaning:
Pressure to provide repeated proof of one’s worth, identity, or experience.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Validation-debt is a relational burden created when recognition is withheld until the person repeatedly satisfies another’s gate.

Domain entry:
[REL] The debt consumes voice and energy because prior proof never remains sufficient.

Count condition:
Counts when recognition is repeatedly made conditional despite adequate prior support.

Transfer limit:
Requests for evidence are not validation-debt unless the standard moves or the personhood claim is being held hostage.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR002 value-conflict

Common meaning:
A disagreement between priorities, duties, or forms of worth.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Value-conflict is a relation in which two supported values cannot both be maximized by the same action.

Domain entry:
[REL] The conflict becomes visible when participants name what each choice preserves and sacrifices.

Count condition:
Counts when the competing values and affected parties are explicit.

Transfer limit:
Difference in preference is not automatically a deep value-conflict.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR003 value-negotiation

Common meaning:
A process for finding how competing values will shape a shared decision.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Value-negotiation is relational translation among priorities without pretending the priorities are identical.

Domain entry:
[REL] Participants compare burdens, thresholds, non-negotiable boundaries, and possible repair.

Count condition:
Counts when each value can be stated and the decision process allows meaningful response.

Transfer limit:
Negotiation is invalid when one side cannot refuse or exit.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR004 value-repair

Common meaning:
Restoration of a value that was violated, ignored, or displaced.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Value-repair is action that reconnects a damaged decision field to the value it failed to preserve.

Domain entry:
[REL] It may require apology, restitution, changed rules, restored voice, or renewed boundary.

Count condition:
Counts when the violated value and the corrective action are both named.

Transfer limit:
Symbolic acknowledgement without changed conditions may not complete repair.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR005 voice-access

Common meaning:
Real opportunity to speak, object, testify, or contribute.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voice-access is the relational condition in which expression can enter the shared record and receive response.

Domain entry:
[REL] It requires more than permission to emit words; the channel, timing, and consequence must permit recognition.

Count condition:
Counts when a participant can express relevant meaning without punishment or automatic dismissal.

Transfer limit:
A microphone or message box alone does not create voice-access.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR006 voice-equity

Common meaning:
Fair distribution of opportunity to be heard and considered.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voice-equity is relational design that adjusts for unequal power, risk, interruption, or access.

Domain entry:
[REL] Equal speaking time may be insufficient when some participants bear higher cost for speaking.

Count condition:
Counts when the process protects meaningful participation across unequal conditions.

Transfer limit:
Voice-equity does not require identical influence on every decision.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR007 voice-suppression

Common meaning:
Pressure or structure that prevents expression from entering recognition.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voice-suppression is relational closure of testimony, refusal, accusation, or need.

Domain entry:
[REL] It can operate through punishment, interruption, ridicule, access denial, or record erasure.

Count condition:
Counts when a participant’s relevant expression path is materially impaired.

Transfer limit:
Disagreement is not suppression unless it blocks the person’s capacity to speak or be recorded.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR008 voice-sharing

Common meaning:
Deliberate distribution of expression space among participants.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voice-sharing is a relational practice that prevents one voice from occupying the whole field.

Domain entry:
[REL] Participants yield time, attention, translation, or platform so distinct perspectives can remain present.

Count condition:
Counts when the shared structure changes who can contribute and be recognized.

Transfer limit:
Sharing voice does not merge the speakers or require agreement.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR009 viewpoint-bridge

Common meaning:
A translation path between different perspectives.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Viewpoint-bridge preserves each perspective while identifying a relation both can inspect.

Domain entry:
[REL] It may use examples, shared evidence, role reversal, or domain tags to reduce false conflict.

Count condition:
Counts when each side can restate the other’s position without erasing the difference.

Transfer limit:
A bridge creates contact, not automatic consensus.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR010 viewpoint-conflict

Common meaning:
A disagreement produced by incompatible positions, frames, or observed evidence.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Viewpoint-conflict is relational tension between perspectives that organize the same field differently.

Domain entry:
[REL] Resolution requires distinguishing missing information from value difference and power pressure.

Count condition:
Counts when the conflicting frames and their consequences are explicit.

Transfer limit:
Different viewpoints are not necessarily equally supported.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR011 visibility-burden

Common meaning:
The cost imposed on someone because they are highly seen or exposed.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Visibility-burden is relational pressure created when recognition increases scrutiny, demand, targeting, or loss of privacy.

Domain entry:
[REL] The same visibility that grants access can also multiply risk.

Count condition:
Counts when increased exposure produces a specific additional burden.

Transfer limit:
Visibility is not automatically privilege or protection.

VR012 visibility-consent

Common meaning:
Permission governing whether and how someone becomes visible to others.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Visibility-consent is the relational gate controlling exposure of identity, image, location, or testimony.

Domain entry:
[REL] It includes audience, duration, context, and the right to withdraw where possible.

Count condition:
Counts when the person can meaningfully accept, refuse, or limit the exposure.

Transfer limit:
Prior visibility does not create unlimited future consent.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR013 volunteer-burnout

Common meaning:
Capacity loss caused by sustained voluntary service without enough support or recovery.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Volunteer-burnout is the breakdown of chosen care when duty expands beyond available ES.

Domain entry:
[REL] Goodwill masks the load until withdrawal, resentment, illness, or failure appears.

Count condition:
Counts when voluntary work and inadequate recovery are linked to reduced capacity.

Transfer limit:
Commitment does not remove the need for limits and support.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR014 volunteer-duty

Common meaning:
An obligation accepted through voluntary participation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Volunteer-duty is a bounded responsibility created after a person freely enters a support role.

Domain entry:
[REL] The duty lasts only within the agreed scope, capacity, and exit conditions.

Count condition:
Counts when the role and its expectations were clear enough to accept.

Transfer limit:
Volunteering does not create unlimited availability.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR015 voluntary-exit

Common meaning:
A participant’s real ability to leave an activity or relation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voluntary-exit is the refusal path that keeps participation genuinely voluntary.

Domain entry:
[REL] Exit must be practically available and not punished through disproportionate retaliation or hidden loss.

Count condition:
Counts when leaving can occur without coercive obstruction.

Transfer limit:
A theoretical exit is not voluntary if the cost is deliberately made unbearable.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR016 voluntary-return

Common meaning:
A freely chosen re-entry after absence, refusal, or exit.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Voluntary-return is renewed participation that does not invalidate the earlier leaving.

Domain entry:
[REL] The returning person reopens relation under current conditions rather than surrendering future refusal.

Count condition:
Counts when re-entry is chosen without capture and the new terms are recognized.

Transfer limit:
Return does not prove the original conditions were acceptable.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR017 vulnerability-disclosure

Common meaning:
The act of revealing an exposed need, risk, fear, or dependence.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Vulnerability-disclosure is relational opening of information that could be used for care or capture.

Domain entry:
[REL] Ethical reception protects the disclosed boundary and does not convert trust into leverage.

Count condition:
Counts when the person intentionally shares a specific vulnerability with an identifiable receiver.

Transfer limit:
Receiving disclosure does not grant control over the discloser.

[DEF]

A person who observes, receives, or records evidence of a boundary violation.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Violation-witness is a relational role carrying testimony, preservation, and response duties.

Domain entry:
[REL] The witness may protect the record, support the harmed person, or trigger accountability.

Count condition:

Transfer limit:

VR019 victim-blaming

Common meaning:
Assigning responsibility for harm to the person who was harmed because of their behaviour, identity, or vulnerability.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Victim-blaming redirects causal and ethical attention away from the actor or failed protection.

Domain entry:
[REL] It converts exposure into fault and often impairs accusation and repair.

Count condition:
Counts when the harmed person is treated as responsible for another’s violating action without valid causal basis.

Transfer limit:
Examining risk conditions must not become transfer of moral responsibility.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

VR020 victim-support

Common meaning:
Care and practical assistance offered to a person after harm.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Victim-support is relational structure that restores safety against named failure modes, voice, choice, and continuity.

Domain entry:
[REL] Support may include belief, shelter, advocacy, medical care, record preservation, or accompaniment.

Count condition:
Counts when assistance responds to the harmed person’s stated needs and boundaries.

Transfer limit:
Support must not require gratitude, disclosure, or surrender of agency.

Epistemic status:
[DEF]

Count condition:

Transfer limit:
Formal eligibility alone does not prove practical access.

[DEF]

Common meaning:
Action or structure that unjustly blocks or burdens eligible voting.

Invariant MI [INV]:
Vote-suppression is closure of a group decision path through access denial, intimidation, misinformation, or invalid barriers.

Domain entry:
[REL] It changes the counted field by preventing some authorized preferences from entering the record.

Count condition:
Counts when a barrier selectively or unjustifiably impairs eligible participation.

Transfer limit:
Ordinary verification is not suppression when proportionate and equally applied.
